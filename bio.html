My Bio

I was born to dirt poor farmers in South Dakota. However, I never accepted them as my parents. In my own imagination, I was a son of God. I was destined for glory. Chasing this dream, I fled from my home and happened upon a crashed green Rolls Royce. The vehicle belonged to an opioid addicted billionaire named Can Dody. I rushed to the damaged green vehicle and pulled the man to safety. As I gazed into his green eyes I realized this was my opportunity. In this instant, I changed my name to “William Daniel Glockner III.” I spent the next several years driving with him around the country. I learned how to fit in with the upper-class and began, at last, to truly belong. Eventually, Can Dody succumbed to his opioid addiction. I had expected to be included in his inheritance, but I was deceived by a woman with a pair of fading, bespectacled eyes. Her eyes had a solemn judging quality to them that would have been perfectly at home on an old advertising billboard.

I was dirt poor, again. I relentlessly chased after my dream and became caught up in a perfectly legitimate business that sold everything except bootlegged opioids. This business gave me the money to throw ludicrously sized parties at my house. I kept hoping that one day a girl that I had known years before would wander into my house, for no reason, and love would, at last, have found me. Despite the infallibility of this plan, I had yet to meet her.

Where did all the money come from… that’s all that Boston wanted to know. I thought wealth would bring friends, in fact, it attracts detractors.

-W
